once as deoxygenated blood (pulmonary circulation) and once as oxygenated blood 
(systemic circulation). Significance: Ensures complete separation of oxygenated and 
deoxygenated blood, allowing efficient oxygen supply to body tissues. [2]

|---------|----------|-------|​

(iii) Sex-linked inheritance: Inheritance of genes located on the sex chromosomes (X or 
Y). Example: Haemophilia, Colour blindness. [2] 
(iv) Haemophilia in males: The gene for haemophilia is located on the X chromosome 
and is recessive. Males have only one X chromosome (XY), so if that X carries the 
defective gene, they will express the disease. Females have two X chromosomes; they 
need both X chromosomes to carry the defective gene to express the disease (rare), 
otherwise they are carriers. [3]
