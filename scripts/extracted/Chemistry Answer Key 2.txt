(i) Modern Periodic Law: The physical and chemical properties of elements are periodic 
functions of their atomic numbers. The modern periodic table has 18 groups and 7 
periods. [2] 

--- PAGE 4 ---
(ii) Element X with atomic number 12:​
(a) Electronic configuration: 2, 8, 2​
(b) Group: 2; Period: 3​
(c) Metal (alkaline earth metal). Formula of oxide: XO (MgO) [3] 
(iii) Trend in ionization potential:​
- Along a period (left to right): Ionization potential increases because nuclear charge 
increases, making it harder to remove an electron.​
- Down a group: Ionization potential decreases because atomic size increases and 
shielding effect increases, making it easier to remove an electron. [3] 
(iv) Electron affinity: The amount of energy released when an atom in the gaseous state 
accepts an electron to form a negative ion. Variation in Group 1: Electron affinity 
decreases down the group (from Li to Cs). [2]

- A round-bottomed flask filled with dry ammonia gas is fitted with a jet tube and 
connected to a beaker of water containing a few drops of red litmus.​
- When a few drops of water are forced into the flask, ammonia dissolves rapidly,

Question 6 [4+2+4=10] 
(i) Haber's Process for ammonia manufacture:​
- Reactants: Nitrogen (from air) and Hydrogen (from water gas or natural gas)​
- Reaction: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ + Heat​
- Conditions:​
- Temperature: 450-500°C​
- Pressure: 200-900 atm​
- Catalyst: Iron (Fe) with promoters like Mo, Al₂O₃, K₂O​
- Unreacted N₂ and H₂ are recycled. [4] 
(ii) Dilute vs Concentrated nitric acid:​
| Property | Dilute HNO₃ | Concentrated HNO₃ |​
|---------|-------------|-------------------|​
| Behaviour | Acts as a typical acid | Acts as a strong oxidizing agent |​
| Reaction with metals | Reacts with metals to produce NO or NO₂ depending on 
concentration | Reacts with metals to produce NO₂ | [2] 
(iii) Balanced equations:​
(a) Conc. HNO₃ with copper: Cu + 4HNO₃ (conc.) → Cu(NO₃)₂ + 2NO₂ + 2H₂O​
(b) HNO₃ with sodium carbonate: Na₂CO₃ + 2HNO₃ → 2NaNO₃ + H₂O + CO₂​
(c) Ammonia with hydrogen chloride: NH₃ + HCl → NH₄Cl (white fumes)​
(d) Thermal decomposition of ammonium chloride: NH₄Cl → NH₃ + HCl (on heating, 
sublimes and recombines) [4]

(i) Metallurgy: The process of extracting a metal from its ore and refining it. 
text 
**Steps in extraction:** 
1. **Crushing and grinding** of the ore 
2. **Concentration (dressing) of the ore:** Removal of gangue by: 
    - Hydraulic washing (gravity separation) 
    - Magnetic separation 
    - Froth flotation process 
3. **Conversion of concentrated ore to metal oxide:**  
    - Roasting (for sulphide ores) in presence of air 
    - Calcination (for carbonate ores) in absence of air 
4. **Reduction of metal oxide to free metal:** 
    - Using reducing agents like carbon, CO, or by electrolysis 
5. **Refining of the metal:** To obtain pure metal (electrorefining) `[4]` 
(ii) Amphoteric oxides: Oxides that react with both acids and bases to form salt and
