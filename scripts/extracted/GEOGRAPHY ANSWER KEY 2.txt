Soil Resources [2+3+3+2=10] 
(i) Alluvial vs Laterite:​
- Alluvial: River deposits, fertile, found in plains.​
- Laterite: Leaching, rich in iron, poor in humus, found in hills. [2] 
(ii) Red Soil:​
(a) Formed by weathering of crystalline and metamorphic rocks (granite, gneiss). [1]​
(b) Crops: Ragi, Groundnut, Tobacco, Millets. (Any two) [1]​
(c) States: Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Odisha. (Any two) [1]

Natural Vegetation [2+3+3+2=10] 
(i) Social forestry: Planting trees on community lands to meet local needs. Importance: 
Fuelwood, fodder, shade, soil conservation. [2] 
(ii) Tidal vs Desert:​
- Tidal: Found in deltas, salt-tolerant, stilted roots (mangroves).​
- Desert: Found in arid regions, thorny, fleshy leaves, long roots. [3] 
(iii) (a) High temperature and rainfall allow multiple layers of vegetation to grow. [1]​
(b) Deciduous forests provide valuable timber like teak and sal. [1]​
(c) Long roots help in absorbing water from deep underground. [1] 
(iv) Trees in Deciduous forests: Teak, Sal, Sandalwood, Peepal, Neem. (Any two) [2]
