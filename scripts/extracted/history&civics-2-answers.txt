Question 3 [3+3+4=10] 
(i) Lok Sabha Composition: Max. 552 members (530 from states, 20 from UTs, 2 
nominated Anglo-Indians - though this provision has lapsed now). Members are directly 
elected by the people from territorial constituencies. [3] 
(ii) Powers of Speaker: Maintains discipline / adjourns the house / presides over joint 
sittings / allows motions like adjournment / final authority on disqualification under 
Anti-Defection Law. (Any three) [3] 
(iii) No-Confidence Motion: A motion moved only in Lok Sabha to test the majority of the 
government. If passed, the government must resign. Significance: Ensures executive 
accountability to the legislature. [4] 
Question 4 [3+3+4=10] 

--- PAGE 3 ---
(i) Judicial Powers of President: Appoints judges of SC and HCs / can grant pardons, 
reprieves, respites or remissions of punishment / is not answerable to any court for 
exercise of powers. [3] 
(ii) Discretionary Powers: Appoints PM when no party has clear majority / can withhold 
assent to bills / can dismiss a government that loses majority but refuses to resign / 
can dissolve Lok Sabha if government loses majority. [3] 
(iii) Collective Responsibility: Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to Lok 
Sabha. Individual Responsibility: Ministers hold office during the pleasure of the 
President, but individually responsible for their department. [4] 
Question 5 [3+3+4=10] 
(i) Supreme Court Composition: 1 Chief Justice + 33 other judges (total 34). Appointed 
by President. [3] 
(ii) Judicial Review: Power to declare a law unconstitutional if it violates the 
Constitution. Importance: Guardian of the Constitution, protects Fundamental Rights. 
[3] 
(iii) District Judge vs Sessions Court: District Judge deals with civil cases; Sessions 
Judge deals with criminal cases. Both are at the same level but handle different types of 
cases. [4] 
Question 6 [3+3+4=10] 
(i) Economic factors for nationalism: Drain of wealth / destruction of Indian industries / 
heavy taxation / exploitation by British. [3] 
(ii) Early Nationalist objectives: Constitutional reforms / administrative reforms / 
economic reforms. Dadabhai Naoroji contributions: Exposed drain of wealth / first 
Indian to become MP in Britain / authored 'Poverty and Un-British Rule in India'. [3] 
(iii) Significance of Partition of Bengal: Led to Swadeshi movement / boycott of British 
goods / growth of assertive nationalism / united Hindus and Muslims temporarily. [4]

Question 8 [2+2+6=10] 
(i) Muslim League founded: 1906. Objective: To protect Muslim interests and loyal to 
British. [2] 
(ii) Lahore Session (1940): Passed the 'Pakistan Resolution' demanding separate 
Muslim state. [2]

League's obstruction / to avoid civil war / immediate transfer of power / fear of greater 
chaos. [6]
